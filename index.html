The path

A long road through circuits, code, and vision systems.

2016

The first embedded experiments

Started learning embedded systems through hands-on experiments with boards, wiring, and firmware. This is where the habit formed: debug the real device, not only the code that should have worked.

2022

Escape-room automation in Kyiv

Built electronics for quest rooms: Arduino controllers, sensors, relays, RFID, light, sound, and automation chains. It was the first place where reliability mattered more than a beautiful prototype.

2023

University, relocation, neural networks, and computer vision

Entered KPI, relocated, and began moving from classic embedded work toward neural networks and computer vision. GoIT gave the first structured push; university projects turned it into deeper practice.

2024

Small commissioned projects and sharper specialization

Took on small commissioned projects where each task mixed firmware, hardware behavior, interfaces, testing, and practical delivery. Good training for turning loose requirements into working devices.

Now

Embedded vision and autonomous systems

Focused on embedded software, Linux boards, computer vision, UAV-related technologies, and navigation without GPS. The interesting zone is where perception, firmware, and physical constraints meet.
